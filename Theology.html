My Experience with the Secret Edition Posssessed Labubu

Posted by eesebaletrainhealthning on August 28, 2025August 28, 2025

By Eesebale Qiu

If you're reading this, I'm already too far gone.

Don't trust the Labubu. I never should've.

When Kasing Lung released that statement "clearing everything up", I thought it was fine. Y'know? It's just based of Norwegian folklore or something. Nothing to do with demons, nope. No paranormal spirits, or demons, ghosts, anything.

Sadly, anyone can lie. I suppose I learnt this the hard way.

The thing about consumerism is that it'll consume us, who we are, what we are. We begin to worship it all, the products, the accessories and all that's unnecessary. Truth is, all this commerce is nothing short of sinful. We'll keep buying things and dumping them into oceans, and when we realise we can't just dump everything into the ocean, we dug a big hole instead and dump our no longer precious dolls and collectibles in there instead. And when that hole is filled up? We dig another. Then we keep going, and wasting, and isn't it just pointless?

So that's the effect of buying too many things and being callous about it. But what about this- you're not wasteful. You buy many things, but you don't throw them away. You keep them clean, shiny, and polished, without so much as a speck of dust on their furry bodies. And you shower them with love, attention, respect. What happens then?

I'll tell you what. You get taken over by it. Nobody should love things so much, whether the love is directed towards the buying of things, the upkeep of things, or the admiring of things.

This is the tragedy of how my life was ruined by my Labubu obsession. Take notes, because you might need them.

During the quarantine, I had, like the sheep I was, gotten the Covid-19 Vaccine. A grave mistake. This was my first exposure to the Autism-19 gene, a gene encoded within the Covid-19 vaccine designed to destroy parts of the brain that Autistic people didn't have so that it essentially gave you Autism. A full year after taking the jab, I realised that I had begun to exhibit signs of neurodivergence.

In late 2024, I fell into yet another trendy trap.

My friends loved to buy these collectible figurines and dolls from this place called Pop Mart. They had set up a small trading circle where, if you got a figure that you didn't want, you could swap it with someone else for a figure you might want a little more.

I wanted to be involved. It felt so lonely to not be participating in the thing practically all of my friends were doing. Thus, I hopped on the collecting bandwagon.

There was a variety of figurines you could collect, and it didn't seem like they had any backstories or lore that I had to catch up on. Among them were Hironos, Skullpandas, Peach Riots, and Crybabies.

But the poor little creature that caught my eye was the Labubu. It was ugly. So ugly, in fact, that it looped back around into being cute. The moment I laid my eyes on it, I felt a strange sense of kinship with this plush toy.

I'm not quite sure what I was thinking at this moment. Maybe I was lamenting our shared attributes. My boyfriend loved to call me an ugly creature, but he also did call me cute often. Maybe I was pissed that my friends didn't try harder to persuade me into joining their little trend. It doesn't make sense objectively speaking, but for me back then it felt like they were taking the chance to engage in activities with each other that didn't involve my presence.

I ended up buying 12 boxes.

Labubu's Connection to Autism

Remember how I said I had contracted a case of lifelong autism? I hope you do, because it's a pretty memorable statement.

Buying 12 boxes of Labubu made my autism flare up like never before. I got what the neurodivergent veterans called a hyperfixation. On Labubus. I would constantly buy new Labubus, hang them up on my bag, or on the ceiling, or tape them to my room's walls.

I had posters of Labubus, my pencilcase was the shape of a Labubu, my toothbrush and pillows were Labubu-themed. I loved to draw and kinda depended on that skill for a extra income, but I had to stop doing requests and commissions because it felt like I couldn't draw anything but Labubus.

When I got repeat copies of the same kind of Labubu, I didn't trade them away or sell them. I was afraid they'd feel unwanted, unnecessary. Just like me. So I built a little table and made miniature porcelain tea sets. I sat all the labubus I wasn't hanging on my bag or on the walls around that table, and let them have a nice tea party.

My social skills also got a lot worse. I couldn't hold a conversation without sneaking in a Labubu reference. I got caught up on the Kasing Lung lore, because of all the figures to get obsessed with I had to choose the one with the concept of a story.

I hardly had time for anything else. I didn't really pay attention to other things, so I didn't have much to say to other people. As a result, my friendships fell apart and my boyfriend broke up with me for being a "freak". I was so lonely that I ended up turning to my Labubus and speaking to them. They wouldn't judge. They wouldn't call me crazy or weird or autistic. They were the only people I could rely on now.

Labubu's Connection to Homosexuality

No, there was one other person. My roommate, the sweetest girl I've ever known. She became my best friend because I had lost all my other best friends during that period of misery, but I just had to ruin it.

It was around Christmas. I was hanging up my Labubu ornaments on my Labubu Christmas Tree whilst crying about something inconsequential when I saw my roommate opening the door and greeting me from under the Labubu mistletoe.

It's not hard to see what happened next. I was vulnerable and in need of affection and I rushed straight towards her and it was never supposed to end up like this.

I'd like to at least say it was enjoyable. But it wasn't because I was crying and wet, and she was caught so off-guard that she could only stare at me in shock as I regained awareness and fled.

I ran without taking my Labubus. I felt a little guilty for leaving my babies, but it was nothing compared to the other disgust I felt with myself.

I moved to Canada and ended up in Saskatchewan, where I found a job and a tiny ran-down apartment to rent.

I then realised something very odd. I was able to work again. Previously, my Labubu obsession had become so debilitating that I was unable to really do anything without thinking of them. But now, the further I got away from them, the more clearheaded I felt, and the worse I also felt.

By then, I had realised the demonic power the Labubu had over people.

Relapsing: My Further Descent Into Madness

I hustled day and night and got a promotion on July two years later. I had no family there (my parents had died in China from a disease that was just revealed as something called the Revenant Pathogen?) and not a lot of acquaintances, so I was wholly focused on work.

My loserism got me a promotion. I readied myself to move back to New York City, where this nightmare had started. I suppose it was fate that I'd have to go back. I felt like a different person already, away from all the Labubus. I could handle this.

I worked there as usual. I got nicer things, better clothes, more spacious accomodations. I went to work using the subway because I was most familiar with it, having used it most of my life alongside my roommate and other friends.

So it shouldn't have been a surprise when, one chilly December morning, I spotted a familiar shock of green hair one crowd away in the subway. I'd thought she'd stopped using the subway because I never saw her there.

There was an illogical sinking feeling in the pit of my stomach. We were practically strangers now, so I shouldn't have felt that way.

Out of curiosity, I followed her without thinking of my original plans. She stepped into a cafe and greeted someone I assumed was her date.

I slipped away, heart pounding, and consulted ChatGPT.

"CHAT!! I feel really lonely... what should I do? Someone I like has moved on or something."

I paraphrased this, but ChatGPT's response was something like "I understand how you feel- sometimes we need to step back and look at the bigger picture. Are you feeling sad because you didn't want that person to move on? In times like this, you should have a reliable emotional support system."

uhoh

Emotional support system. Yes, that's right. My emotional support system has always been Labubus.

I had been so lifeless and unhappy before, despite having a good job and convincing myself I'd moved on.

I wanted my Labubus back.

So I wanted to sneak into the apartment we used to share to get them, but first I had to check the dumpsters in case I was lucky and she'd dumped them there so I wouldn't have to break in.

It had been two years, so maybe they were already in the ocean or some rubbish dump area or something.

My last remaining shreds of sanity and the insecurity I had that told me she wouldn't have wasted time in throwing away or selling everything I'd left made me walk towards the nearest Pop Mart and purchase 50 boxes of Labubus.

And then the voices started coming.

Every night I would hear her voice. Slowly, it transitioned into the cute voices of Labubus, going "Labubu! Labubu! We're going to take over the world with our mind control powers and we're going to create so much plastic waste the world burns so that our dark master will have the opportunity to arise again and grace us with his presence and then we'll recreate hell on Earth!"

So at this point I'm unable to work. I got fired and couldn't pay the rent. I'm now in the middle of the forest trying to catch a deer with my bare hands. Hopefully I can survive a few more days by eating the Labubus I still have.
